The Math Behind the Returns

Why Do These Cash-Flow Numbers Look So High?

Most investors see these returns and assume they're inflated. They're not — the difference is the amortization. A conventional residential mortgage runs on a 25-year schedule, producing higher monthly payments that compress cash flow. CMHC MLI Select allows up to a 50-year amortization on qualifying purpose-built rental builds. At a $2M mortgage, that single structural difference reduces your monthly debt service by approximately $2,500–$3,000. DSCR improves because your monthly debt obligation shrinks — not because the income is inflated. The rents are market-rate. The cash flow is real. It's the product of a government-backed program built specifically to incentivize purpose-built rental housing in Canada.

$0 in Sales Tax — Alberta Advantage
Alberta has no provincial sales tax (no HST). Under the federal government's September 2023 Excise Tax Act amendment, new purpose-built rental housing of 4+ units qualifies for a full federal GST rebate. These properties qualify on both counts. Combined: $0 in net sales tax on purchase — a saving of up to 5% of purchase price compared to new construction in other provinces. The GST rebate is subject to CRA eligibility conditions including a long-term rental hold requirement. Consult your accountant.

Qualification Requirements

This Program Isn't for Everyone

CMHC MLI Select has strict qualification criteria. We'd rather you know now than find out at application stage. If you meet these requirements, you're exactly who this is built for.

5% Minimum Deposit
You must put a minimum of 5% down at time of purchase. Budget an additional 2–3% for closing costs — total cash to close is typically 7–8% of the purchase price.
10% Liquidity Requirement
After the deposit clears, you must demonstrate liquid assets (cash, GICs, securities) equal to 10% of the project price. This stays in your account — it's a CMHC verification requirement, not an additional payment.
25% Net Worth of Project Price
Your total net worth (assets minus liabilities) must equal at least 25% of the purchase price. On a $2M project, that means $500K minimum. Real estate equity, investments, and business assets all count.
Prior Landlord Experience Required
CMHC requires documented rental income history. First-time homeowners with no landlord record do not qualify. At least one rental property — including a basement or secondary suite — satisfies this requirement.
Minimum 680 Credit Score
CMHC and the insured lender will pull your credit bureau. A minimum score of approximately 680 is required. Recent collections, active consumer proposals, or undischarged bankruptcies disqualify an application.
Long-Term Rental Commitment
The property must be held as long-term market rental. Condo conversions, short-term rental platforms, or resale within the holding period violate CMHC program terms and trigger recapture of the insurance benefit.
If you meet these criteria, the discovery call is where we verify your eligibility, run your specific numbers, and confirm fit — before you commit to anything. No pressure. No obligation. Just clarity.
