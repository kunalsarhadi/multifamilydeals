Anatomy of a Completed Edmonton 8-Plex: The Crawford Plains Project

By Kunal Sarhadi, Real Estate Broker July 2026 7 min read
The Short Version

The Crawford Plains project is an eight-unit purpose-built rental in south Edmonton, delivered in July 2026 — turnkey, with professional property management in place from day one. Its interior specification is the refined second iteration of the standard set by our Inglewood 9-plex, and at financing it was independently appraised $100,000 above its purchase price by an AACI-designated appraiser. This case study walks through what "completed and delivered" actually looks like, unit by unit and document by document.

Most multi-family marketing shows you renderings. This article does the opposite: it walks through a building that is finished, appraised, managed, and delivered — the Crawford Plains 8-plex in south Edmonton, handed over in July 2026. If you are evaluating a package in our current inventory, this is the most concrete preview available of what you would be buying: not a concept, but the standard our completed projects are actually built and delivered to.

The Building: Eight Units, Purpose-Built From the Foundation Up

Crawford Plains is a purpose-built rental — designed as an income asset from the first drawing, not a house adapted into one. The eight units are organized in the two-tier structure our buildings use: two upper suites paired with lower suites, each unit fully self-contained. That structure matters for more than layout. Purpose-built construction is what qualifies these projects for CMHC MLI Select in the first place, and it is what appraisers and lenders treat as institutional-grade rental product rather than an improvised conversion.

The neighbourhood does quiet work too. Crawford Plains is an established community in south Edmonton — existing amenities, schools, and transit, with the tenant demand profile of a mature area rather than a speculative edge-of-city bet. It is the same neighbourhood logic that runs through our whole track record.

The Iteration Story: What Inglewood Taught Us

Our Inglewood 9-plex, completed in 2026, set the original interior standard. Crawford Plains is the second iteration — the version where the lessons from operating a completed building got fed back into the specification. The interior finish package was upgraded and refined from the Inglewood standard: the choices that proved durable and leasable stayed, and the details that could be better were revised.

That iteration loop is the point of buying from a builder-led program rather than a one-off project. Each completed building becomes the quality-control baseline for the next one, and buyers of later projects inherit the accumulated refinements without paying a prototype's learning curve.

The Appraisal: +$100,000 at Completion

At financing in 2025, the project was valued by an AACI-designated appraiser in an as-if-complete appraisal — the independent valuation a lender relies on to advance mortgage funds. The conclusion: $100,000 above the purchase price.

We publish the delta rather than the underlying figures, out of respect for the buyer's privacy — but the meaning of the delta is straightforward. An independent professional, working for the lender's file, concluded that the buyer had contracted the asset below its market value at completion. That result is not unique to this building: every completed project we have delivered has appraised above its purchase price, a pattern we break down fully in our appraisal track record analysis.

Why This Matters to the Next Buyer

A completed project's appraisal is the closest thing a prospective buyer gets to third-party evidence about a builder's pricing discipline. Crawford Plains was priced during construction, delivered on standard, and independently valued above its contract price — the same process now running on the packages in our current inventory.

"Turnkey" Means Managed, Not Just Finished

Delivery in July 2026 did not mean handing over keys and a construction file. The building was delivered turnkey with professional property management already in place — the operational layer that turns a finished structure into a functioning income asset. For an out-of-province investor, this is the difference between owning an Edmonton building and running one: leasing, tenant relations, and day-to-day operations are handled by a professional manager from day one.

That is the operating model across our program, and it is why investors in Ontario and BC can hold Edmonton multi-family without relocating their lives to do it.
